The CRAFT framework (Context, Role, Action, Format, Tone/Target) creates precise AI prompts by defining background, persona, specific tasks, output structure, and style, ensuring high-quality, actionable results. Using this method reduces ambiguity, prevents "hallucinations," and aligns AI output with specific needs by providing constraints and detailed instructions. 
---

The C.R.A.F.T. Prompting Method
Context (What/Why): Provide background information, such as the situation, relevant data, or why you need the task done. This includes who, what, where, and when.
Role (Persona): Assign a specific persona or expertise to the AI, such as "You are an expert content marketer" or "You are a Python developer," to anchor its perspective.
Action (Task): Define the exact verb-driven task you want performed (e.g., "summarize," "write," "code," "analyze"), ensuring it is specific rather than vague.
Format (Output): Specify how the output should look, such as "a markdown table," "three bullet points," "a JSON object," or "a 500-word essay".
Tone/Target (Style/Audience): Define the voice (e.g., formal, witty, empathetic) and the intended audience (e.g., beginners, C-suite executives). 
---

Key Rules for Effective Prompting
Be Specific: Vague prompts fail. Precise, detailed instructions yield the best results.
Use Constraints: Limit the output, such as banning specific words, capping the word count, or setting a specific persona constraint.
Iterate and Refine: Run the prompt, review the results, and then refine the prompt to improve the output.
Use Examples (Few-Shot): Providing one or two examples of desired output dramatically improves AI accuracy.
Structure Your Input: Use markdown headers or XML tags (e.g., <background> or ### Instructions) to help the AI distinguish between constraints and tasks.
