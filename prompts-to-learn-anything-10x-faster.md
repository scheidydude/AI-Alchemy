# Prompts to Learn Anything 10x Faster

## Explain like I’m 5

> Summarize [topic] in the simplest terms possible, like you’re explaining it to a 5-year-old.

## Visualize the process

> Create a step-by-step mental image or visual map of how [concept] works.

## Break it into chunks

> Divide [topic] into 3-5 main parts and explain each part briefly.

## Find the patterns

> What patterns, formulas, or rules can I extract from [topic] to remember it better?

## Use analogies

> Compare [topic] to something familiar or unrelated to make it easier to understand.

## Break myths

> What are three misconceptions about [topic], and what’s the truth behind them?

## Relate to real life

> Explain how [topic] connects to daily life or a practical situation I might encounter.

## Teach it back

> How would I teach [topic] to someone who knows nothing about it?

## Ask the critical ‘why’

> Why does [concept] matter, and what are its key implications in [field]?

## Simulate or practice

> Give me a simple example, scenario, or exercise to apply [topic] right now.

## Turn it into a story

> Write a short story or scenario where [topic] is applied in a relatable context.

## Challenge it

> What are the common misunderstandings or mistakes people make about [topic], and how do I avoid them?

## Prioritize learning

> What are the 2–3 most important concepts in [topic] I should focus on first?

## Find the gaps

> What are the most overlooked aspects of [topic] that are crucial to understanding it?
